Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

1931 – 2015

Missile Man of India

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam (15 October 1931 – 27 July 2015) was an Indian aerospace scientist who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, he studied physics and aerospace engineering. He worked for four decades as a scientist and administrator at DRDO and ISRO, playing a central role in India's space and missile programs. Known as the “Missile Man of India,” he also contributed to the Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998.
